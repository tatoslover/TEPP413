Background

I am a student teacher from the University of Canterbury, teaching at Ellesmere College for 6 weeks (20/4/26-29/5/26).

I grew up in Palmerston North and attended PNBHS and WBHS. I generally did well at school, particularly with Statistics. However, I struggled with the pressures of being a teenager. I was uncertain of my place in the world, had no idea what I wanted, and tended to overthink everything. My thinking was very black-and-white and I had poor resilience when it came to problem solving. I hope to inspire young people to understand themselves and what they want to get out of life.

Here is me aged 13; I only grew 3 inches in high school. I represented my schools in basketball, rowing, swimming, and water polo alongside representing New Zealand in both swimming and basketball.

Teaching runs in my family. My grandmother, mother, and sister all taught at high schools. After gaining a lot of life experience, I believe I can teach the next generation of Kiwis.

Teaching philosophy

I struggled to understand why we learn so much in school that seems irrelevant to everyday life. The answer I have come to: high school teaches us how to think and how to learn. If you can build a genuine passion for improving, you will succeed. Be flexible, try things, see what works, and ask for help when you are stuck.

I want to help you develop your own worldview and style. Think carefully about what you enjoy and what you avoid. Be honest with yourself. If you fail an assignment or a class, it is an opportunity to learn about yourself and grow.

An idea about maths: everything can be data, and we are all data-processing machines. Every experience, every decision, every habit is an input that shapes your output. The question is whether you are directing that processing toward things that help you grow, or just letting it run on autopilot.

School, and maths in particular, is one of the best places to practise thinking deliberately. I hope you come to see maths as a language for understanding the world. After all, how would we communicate with aliens? Maths is a universal language.

What you can expect from me

  • I will focus on the why behind what we are learning, not just the how
  • I will be honest and transparent with you
  • I will make many mistakes but will endeavour to learn from them to improve my skills
  • I will always treat you with respect and listen to your ideas
  • We will have fun
